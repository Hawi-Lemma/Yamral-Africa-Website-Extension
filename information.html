ETHIOPIA

Ethiopia is one of the most distinct and exceptional countries in the world. With over 102 million inhabitants, it is the most populous landlocked country globally, and interestingly, contains ample skeletal evidence for anatomically modern humans. What Ethiopia lacks in modern tourist infrastructure, it more than makes up for with culture, scenery, and sights aplenty. Thousands of monasteries and churches occupy Ethiopia’s extensive 420,000 square miles and is a celebration of the country’s ancient Orthodox Christianity, a vital part of life for its people. With three different climate zones according to elevation, Ethiopia is home to lush highlands, sweeping lakes, and exhilarating volcanoes. Equally impressive experiences include partaking in the local traditional culture and seeing an abundance of wildlife.

Banking And Currency
Currency

The unit of currency in Ethiopia is Ethiopian Birr(ETB). There are 100, 50,10 and 5 Birr notes. Then there is 1 Birr, 50c, 25c,10c and 5c coins. USD and other major currencies can be changed at any bank available.

Banking

Local currency is easily obtained using credit card or debit card from the widely available ATMs. Banks are usually open between 08:30 - 16:00 Mon to Sat. Withdrawals are limited to a USD 300-400 daily total. It is advised to carry some hard currency cash just incase an ATM network is not working.

Travel, Transport And Getting Around

Travel by air is best with Ethiopian Airlines that has a good network of domestic flights connecting Addis Ababa to most major tourist destinations. Atleast on flight goes in either direction between any combination of Addis Ababa, Bahir Dar, Gondar, Lalibela and Axum. There are also flights to Arba Minch, Jinka, Kombolcha, Gambella, Jimma, Assosa, Jijiga and Dire Dewa.

Travel by bus is available in either direction between Addis Ababa and Bahir Dar, Gondar, Mekele, Axum, Dessie, Hawassa, Jimma, Nekemte and Assosa. A good chose of reasonably priced private buses are available with daily fixed departures.

Travel by Taxi and Bajaje(three wheeled tuk-tuks) is available in many large towns. These can be used to move around areas within the towns.

Food and Drinks

A wide variety of different local dishes are available in Ethiopia. The staple source of carbohydrates in Ethiopia is Injera. If you dine in a group it is customary for everyone to eat off the same plate. In addition many local restaurants serve western-food with the selection being best in Addis Ababa.

An attractive culinary feature in Ethiopia is the numerous pastry shops and cafes that serve a selection of iced and plain cakes along with coffee, tea and fruit juice. Ethiopia being the origin of coffee, coffee forms a large part of the culture. The local espresso-style coffee can be found at most local food restaurants. For a more western experiance of Ethiopia's coffee one can visit the exclusive coffee shops spread across the city.

Weather and Climate

Ethiopia shows a wide climatic variation, ranging from the peaks of Bale, which receive periodic snowfall, to regular daytime tempratures of over 50°C in the Danakil Desert. The central highlands have a temperate climate and average day time temperature of 16°C. The eastern lowlands and and the far south are dry and hot. The western lowlands are moist and hot with a truly tropical. The southern rift valley is temperate to hot and seasonally moist.


Language

Some 70 languages are spoken in Ethiopia. Most of the languages belong to the Semitic or Cushitic branches of the Afro-Asiatic family.Some of the Semitic languagges are Amharigna, Tigrigna and Guragegna. Some of the Cushitic languages are Oromifa and the Somali Language. In the Omo River Vally a group of Omotic languages, closely related to the Cushitic language group, are sponken. Along the western border with South-Sudan languages are mostly of the unrelated Nilotic group. Amharigna is the official language of Ethiopia.
