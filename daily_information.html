Day 1 : Addis Ababa Sheraton Addis / Ethiopian Sky Light Hotel
ACTIVITIES AND SERVICES
×
Welcoming

Yamral Africa’s representative will welcome you at Bole International Airport in Addis Ababa. You will then drive to your hotel. If your flight arrives in the morning, time can be made for sightseeing in and around Addis Ababa. You will stay the night in Addis Ababa at Sheraton Addis / Ethiopian Skylight Hotel.

×
National Museum

The national museum is located near the Addis Ababa University. It contains Archaeological exhibits and artifacts dating back to the pre-Axumite civilization.

×
Unity Park

The newly opened park is situated on the 20 out of the 40ha compound where the Office of the Prime Minister is. The park contains historical buildings, indigenous plants exhibition, indigenous black mane lion zoo, a green area and the nine regions’ pavilion. The park also features more than 1000 items on display.

Day 2 : Bahir Dar Kuriftu Resotr and Spa / Avanti Blue Nile Hotel
ACTIVITIES AND SERVICES
×
Drive

After an early breakfast you will start driving to Bahir Dar. En route you will visit Debre Libanos monastery and continue to Bahir Dar.

×
Debre Libanos

Debre Libanos is an important monastic center for Ethiopian Orthodox Christianity. The monastery complex sits on a terrace between a cliff and the gorge of one of the tributaries of the Nile river. It has an attractive marble interior and stained glass windows.

Day 3 : Bahir Dar Kuriftu Resotr and Spa / Avanti Blue Nile Hotel
ACTIVITIES AND SERVICES
×
Boat excursion and Drive

In the morning you will be set for a breathtaking boat excursion on Lake Tana to visit isolated monasteries of Ura Kidane Meheret and Azwa Mariam, famous for their ancient manuscripts, crosses, icons, and paintings. Birdwatching is a popular activity considering the lush forest surrounding much of the lake.
After lunch you will drive some 45 minutes on a bumpy road to the village of Tis Isat to see the Blue Nile Falls.

×
Ura Kidane Miheret

This most frequently visited beautiful church was built in the 16th century. Its maqdas is covered in a stunning jumble of murals painted between 250 and 100 years ago. The paintings give a glimpse into medieval Ethiopia. A separate museum holds old crowns of Ethiopian Kings, leather bound bibles and other ancient artifacts. The walk to the church has an adventurous ambiance with the sound of the trees shaken by Vervet Monkeys and butterflies fluttering over small pools. Birds such as parrots and hornbills can be spotted in the tress.

×
Azawa Mariam

Azawa Mariam has a thatch roof atop the church giving it make it a unique look. It is adorned with several 18th century paintings and an attached treasury house containing antiques.

×
Blue Nile Falls

Originating at Lake Tana, the Blue Nile Falls is one of Africa’s most spectacular waterfalls plunging over a 45m high rock face and locally known as Tis Isat. The construction of a new hydroelectric dam has diverted much of the water for this the falls’ volume could be very minimal at ceratain times through the year. Accordingly, The 160m wide falls is best enjoyed in the rainy season (May to October), when the thunderous sound can be heard from far. Its length is about 907 miles (1,460 km) and joins the While Nile in Khartoum forming the longest river in the world – The Nile River.

Day 4 : Gondar Goha Hotel / Zobel Resort Hotel
ACTIVITIES AND SERVICES
×
Drive

After an early breakfast you will drive to Gondar. After checking into your hotel, you will set for a tour of Gondar, a town founded in 1636 by the great Emperor Fassiladas as a royal capital of Ethiopia. You will visit the Fasiladas Compound, a UNESCO World Heritage site where enchanting 16th-century castles stand. Later you will proceed to the fascinating Debre Birhan Selassie Church.

×
Fassil Compound

Fassil Ghebbi (meaning ‘fassil compound’ in the Amharic language) is found in the heart of the city of Gondar. The compound is founded on a 7 hectare stone wall enclosure containing six stone castles and several minor buildings. Some of the most significant are Fasilidas’s Castle , Yohannes’s library and Iyasu’s Castle.

×
Debre Birhan Selassie

Debre Birhan Selassie Church lies 1km from the town center. It was considered Gondar's main center of worship for atleast a century. Beautiful art graces every corner of the ceiling and walls.

Day 5 : Simien Mountain National Park Simien Lodge
ACTIVITIES AND SERVICES
×
Drive

Set in for a drive to the jagged panorama of the Simien Mountains National Park, the park encompasses the northwestern side of the Simien Mountains, a unique massif ranging from 1,900m to the RasDashen peak, at 4,533m the highest in Ethiopia.

It is home to some of Africa’s most striking wildlife, Gelada baboon, Walia Ibex and the exceedingly rare Ethiopian Wolf. The park is also renowned for her incredible biodiversity with over 1,200 species of plants 3 of which are endemic to the Simien Mountains. You will stay night at the Simien Lodge.

Day 6 : Axum Yeha Hotel / Armah Hotel
ACTIVITIES AND SERVICES
×
Tekeze River Valley

Leaving the Simien Mountains National park, you will drive 350km to Axum passing through the spectacular canyon of the Tekeze River valley—in the most intimate ways possible. You will have lunch on the way and arrive at Axum late in the afternoon.

The ancient city of Axum (also spelt Aksum), siting at an elevation of 2130m on the Tigrayan plateau, symbolizes the wealth and importance of the civilization of the ancient Aksumite kingdom, which lasted from the 1st to the 8th centuries AD. The city hosts the countries first Orthodox church and boasts many astonishing archeological relicts.

Day 7 : Axum Yeha Hotel / Armah Hotel
ACTIVITIES AND SERVICES
×
Stelae Park

Your full day city tour of Axum will include the Stelae Park a site for the famed obelisks with a UNESCO World Heritage Site status. These royal tombs and the Palace ruins date back to the 6th and 7th centuries AD. The largest standing obelisk rises to a height of over 23 meters and is superbly carved to represent a nine-story building of the Aksumites. The largest obelisk of some 33 meters long lies where it fell, possibly the largest monolithic stele that ancient human beings ever attempted to erect.

×
St. Mary’s Church

Continuing the tour you will visit St. Mary’s Church and the Sanctuary Chapel which is the repository of the Ark of the Covenant. It was established in the 4th century by King Ezena.

Day 8 : Mekelle Zemarias Hotel / Hotel Mekele
ACTIVITIES AND SERVICES
×
Drive

This day you will drive to Mekele visiting en route Yeha. Lunch will be in Adigrat on the road to mekele.Then you will continue driving to Mekele and you will stay the night in Mekele.

×
Yeha

Yeha is the most important pre-Axumite site and perhaps Ethiopia’s oldest capital. As Ethiopia’s first known capital in the 5th century BC, Yeha claims to be the birthplace of the country’s earliest civilization. The pre-Christian temple, which is believed to be the oldest standing building in the country, and the Great Palace ruins at Yeha has massive walls and displays incredible skills in stonework which date back to about seven or eight hundred years before the birth of Christ.

Drive

You will set early in the morning to your drive to Lalibela, known for its distinctive rock-cut churches dating from the 12th and 13th centuries. There will be some sightseeing enroute. And lunch will be in one of the towns on the way. You will stay the night in Lalibela.

Day 10 : Lalibela Mezena Lodge
ACTIVITIES AND SERVICES
×
Sightseeing | Lalibela Churches

After a relaxed breakfast in your hotel you will visit the churches which are the most famous and arguably Ethiopian’s topmost attraction. The churches are carved below ground level and ringed by trenches and courtyards. They are connected to each other by a tangled maze of tunnels and passages. Among the churches you will visit are Bete Amanuel, Bete Markorios , and Bete Giorgis - the most majestic and best preserved of all Lalibela’s churches. You will stay the night in Lalibela

Day 11 : Lalibela Mezena Lodge
ACTIVITIES AND SERVICES
×
Nakuto Leab

In the morning you will take an excursion to Nakute Le’Abe, a monastery just four miles from Lalibela. This monastery is named after King Nakuto Leab who abdicated his throne in 1280 A.D. and went to a cave to lead a hermit’s life. Ever since, the cave has been a monastery consisting of a simple church built around a shallow cave in which several holy pools are fed by natural springs. The church has many treasures including paintings crosses and an illuminated leather bible. You will then drive back to Lalibela and have the rest of the day at leisure. You will stay the night in Lalibela.
